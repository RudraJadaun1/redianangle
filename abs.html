What is a Radian?

A radian is a unit of angle based entirely on the circle's own proportions. Instead of arbitrarily dividing a circle into 360 degrees, radians measure angles by looking at the edge.

If you take the Radius (the distance from the center to the edge) and bend it along the circle's curve, the distance it covers is the Arc Length.

The angle created when Arc Length exactly equals the Radius is exactly 1 Radian.

Angle (θ) = Arc Length (s) / Radius (r)
